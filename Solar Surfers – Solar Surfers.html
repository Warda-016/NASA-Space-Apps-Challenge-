ASTEROIDS

Asteroids are remnants from the early stages of the solar system, formed about 4.6 billion years ago from the leftover materials that did not coalesce into planets.

Discovery

The first asteroid, Ceres, was discovered by Italian astronomer Giuseppe Piazzi in 1801. Initially classified as a planet, it eventually led to the recognition of a new category of celestial objects.

The term “asteroid” comes from the Greek word “asteroeides,” meaning star-like, reflecting their small, rocky appearance compared to planets.

Impacts on Earth

Asteroids have had a significant impact.

Especially, on Earth’s geological and biological history. Major collisions, such as the one believed to have caused the extinction of the dinosaurs about 65 million years ago, have shaped the evolution of life on our planet.
While most asteroids reside in the asteroid belt, some venture closer to Earth. These Near-Earth Objects (NEOs) have captured scientists’ attention due to their potential impact risk
